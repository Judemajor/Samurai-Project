Timeline

Aug 27, 646

Taika reform

"This order allowed the Japanese aristocracy to adopt the Tang dynasty political structure, bureaucracy, culture, religion, and philosophy."

Nov 15, 797

Birth of Samurai

"Some clans were originally formed by farmers who had taken up arms to protect themselves from the Imperial magistrates sent to govern their lands and collect taxes. These clans formed alliances to protect themselves against more powerful clans". this created the warriors known as Samurai, so the Bushido code established as a result.

Jun 15, 795

Introduction of Shogun

This is when the shogun was introduced and they employed samurai to combat rebellion and any other enemy.

Jan 19, 1160

Heiji rebellion

The Minamoto and Taira clans were put against each other during this rebellion that would spark a civil war.

Apr 25, 1185

Gempei war

This major war between the Minamoto and Taira decided who would control Japan. A major battle was at Dan-no-ura, the battle and war ended with the Minamoto being victorious and the Kamakura shogunate being established.

Nov 28, 1274

First Mongol invasion

"The Mongol-founded Yuan dynasty sent a force of some 40,000 men and 900 ships to invade Japan in Kyushu". The force of 10,000 samurai, as well as major thunderstorms, caused major Mongol casualties and the Mongols retreated.

Nov 28, 1281

Second Mongol invasion

In the second invasion while the samurai protected their land honorably, a typhoon hit that would take out the majority of Mongol forces. Causing them to retreat a second time.

1336

Establishment of Ashikaga Shogunate

After the invasions, a rebellion led by Ashikaga Takauji caused the fall of the Kamakura Shogunate. But after the establishment of the Ashikaga shogunate, constant wars ensued with different clans fighting to take control of Japan.

1467-1477

Onin War

After this war the Ashikaga shogunate fell and "local lords and their samurai stepped in to a greater extent to maintain law and order"

1588

Sword carry rights

A law was created forbidding non-samurai from carrying weapons. The samurai class had become hereditary at this point, so those of a lower social class struggled.

1573-1603

Moyama period

The warlord Oda Nobunaga overthrew the current Japanese government, and that began the Moyama period.

1615

Unification of Japan

Japan entered a 250-year period of peace and prosperity while under Tokugawa Ieyasu. "In a peaceful Japan, many samurai were forced to become bureaucrats or take up some type of trade, even as they preserved their conception of themselves as fighting men. "

1868

Meiji Restoration

The Choshu and Satsuma clans joined together to overthrow the Tokugawa shogunate. This restoration would start the decline of samurai.

1870's

End of Samurai era

In 1871, feudalism was abolished. Then in 1876, wearing swords was forbidden unless you were part of the National Armed forces. Throughout the 1870's there were small samurai rebellions but were quickly shut down by the army.
